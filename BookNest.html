BOOKNEST- An app designed for booklovers.

The Book Club App is a perfect place to discover and discuss books of all kinds, with anyone, anywhere. With everything going on in the world, you will be able to create groups, meet with Authors, and discuss the ins and outs of your favorite books virtually. This app will lend its hand to the busy people who are still interested in having a sense of belonging by having access to cliff notes and personal notes from the last meeting, as well as having access to Goodreads.

The Problem

In this busy world book lovers, love to attend bookclub meetings and talk about the book that they have read. But they are unable to attend those due to several reasons like busy schedule and lack of transportation.

The Solution

To rescue from the problem, I designed an app which is lite and is easily accessible everywhere and anytime. By including this app in their daily life, the readers can to cope up with the bookclub meetings, get recommendations on various books to read and make new friends to share their book reviews.

Design Process

Survey

I used google forms to create the survey and send it out to various readers and non-readers and gathered the data as shown below. When interviewing and surveying our users, we found that a lot of the people wanted to join a book club but could not find the time to do so. Users also did not have the knowledge or resources to start/join a book club on their own. Majority of the users prefer Fiction over Non-Fiction 50% of users listen to audiobooks while the other 50% read physical books

User Interview Questions

  1. How many questions would you like provided for a guide?
  2. Would you prefer a brief discussion guide or something that has more detailed questions?
  3. Do you prefer weekly prompts and guides or monthly? Why do you prefer that?
  4. Did you have any negative experiences about the meet-ups?
  5. If yes, What kind of negative experiences do you have?
  6. What platforms do you use? Zoom? FT? Google Meetings?
  7. Do you prefer real time conversations or chats or not in real time? Why?
  8. Where would be the best place to meet up with a group?
  9. How do you choose what to read next?
  10. What friends have more weight? Work friends, personal friends?
  11. Do you care what celebrities are reading? Reese/Oprah/Brad Pitt?
  12. What is your preferred genre to read?
  13. What would it take to get you to read outside of your favored genre?
  14. What genre do you not like to read at all?
